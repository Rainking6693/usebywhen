🦃 Thanksgiving Food Storage

Safe storage for turkey, sides, and all your Thanksgiving favorites

🦃 Roasted Turkey

3-4 days refrigerated

Whole turkey: Carve within 2 hours

Sliced turkey: Store in shallow containers

Gravy: 1-2 days refrigerated

Freezing: 2-6 months for best quality

Reheating: Heat to 165°F internal temperature

🥔 Mashed Potatoes

3-5 days refrigerated

Storage: Airtight containers

Reheating: Add milk/butter, heat thoroughly

Freezing: 10-12 months (texture changes)

Safety: Don't leave out > 2 hours

🥧 Pumpkin Pie

3-4 days refrigerated

Storage: Cover and refrigerate

Room temp: Max 2 hours

Freezing: 1-2 months

Note: Custard-based, needs refrigeration

🍠 Sweet Potato Casserole

3-5 days refrigerated

With marshmallows: 3-4 days

Without toppings: 4-5 days

Freezing: 2-3 months

Reheating: 350°F until heated through

🦴 Turkey Stock/Broth

3-4 days refrigerated

Homemade: 3-4 days refrigerated

Freezing: 4-6 months

Storage: Cool quickly, refrigerate promptly

Uses: Soups, gravies, cooking liquid

🥗 Green Bean Casserole

3-5 days refrigerated

Storage: Airtight containers

Reheating: 350°F covered

Freezing: Not recommended (texture issues)

Fresh toppings: Add when reheating

🎄 Christmas Food Storage

Storage guidelines for ham, cookies, and all your Christmas treats

🍖 Glazed Ham

3-5 days refrigerated

Whole ham: Slice for easier storage

Spiral cut: 3-5 days refrigerated

Freezing: 1-2 months

Reheating: 325°F until heated through

Glaze: Store separately if possible

🍪 Christmas Cookies

1-2 weeks room temperature

Decorated cookies: 1 week room temp

Plain cookies: 2-3 weeks room temp

Freezing: 2-3 months

Storage: Airtight containers

Tip: Layer with parchment paper

🍰 Christmas Cake

1 week room temp, 2 weeks refrigerated

Fruitcake: 1 month room temp

Pound cake: 3-4 days room temp

Frosted cake: Refrigerate

Freezing: 2-4 months

🥛 Eggnog

5-7 days refrigerated

Homemade: 2-3 days refrigerated

Store-bought: 5-7 days after opening

Unopened: Check expiration date

Freezing: Not recommended

🍞 Christmas Bread

3-5 days room temperature

Stollen: 1-2 weeks room temp

Panettone: 1 week room temp

Cranberry bread: 3-4 days

Freezing: 2-3 months

🍫 Holiday Candy

Varies by type

Chocolate: 6-12 months

Hard candy: 12 months

Fudge: 2-3 weeks room temp

Candy canes: 12 months

🎊 New Year's Food Storage

Party foods, appetizers, and celebration treats storage guide

🥂 Champagne & Wine

3-5 days after opening

Champagne: 1-3 days with stopper

White wine: 3-5 days refrigerated

Red wine: 3-5 days cool, dark place

Unopened: Store properly for years

🧀 Cheese Platters

Varies by cheese type

Hard cheese: 3-4 weeks

Soft cheese: 1-2 weeks

Fresh cheese: 3-7 days

Storage: Wrap individually

🦐 Shrimp Cocktail

1-2 days refrigerated

Cooked shrimp: 3-4 days

With sauce: 1-2 days

Storage: Keep on ice

Safety: Don't leave out > 2 hours

🥖 Appetizer Spreads

3-5 days refrigerated

Hummus: 3-5 days

Spinach dip: 3-4 days

Cream cheese based: 3-5 days

Storage: Airtight containers

🛡️ Holiday Food Safety Tips

🌡️ Temperature Control

  • Keep hot foods hot (140°F+)
  • Keep cold foods cold (40°F or below)
  • Use food thermometers
  • Don't leave food out > 2 hours
  • 1 hour if temp > 90°F

🧊 Proper Storage

  • Cool foods quickly before refrigerating
  • Use shallow containers for faster cooling
  • Label containers with dates
  • Store raw and cooked foods separately
  • Use oldest items first

🔥 Reheating Safety

  • Heat leftovers to 165°F
  • Stir foods while reheating
  • Don't reheat foods more than once
  • Use microwave-safe containers
  • Let food rest after microwaving

🧽 Cleanliness

  • Wash hands frequently
  • Clean surfaces and utensils
  • Use separate cutting boards
  • Don't cross-contaminate
  • Replace sponges regularly

👥 Large Gatherings

  • Plan refrigerator space ahead
  • Use coolers with ice for extra space
  • Serve food in small batches
  • Replace serving utensils regularly
  • Monitor food temperatures

📅 Planning Ahead

  • Prep foods that store well
  • Freeze items in advance
  • Make shopping lists by storage needs
  • Plan leftover meals
  • Have storage containers ready
